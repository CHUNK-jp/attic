Why I built this
The developer
@chunk_jp

A few years ago I got tired of tools that wanted access to everything just to show me a search bar. Google Drive, Notion, Dropbox — they all promised to help me find my files, but the price was always the same: hand over your data, trust a third-party server, sign into one more account. I didn't want that trade.

The moment I decided to build Attic was after a frustrating afternoon trying to track down a contract PDF. I knew it was on my Mac. Spotlight couldn't find it. The cloud search tool I'd been using had indexed the folder wrong. I spent twenty minutes looking for something I had made myself, on hardware I owned. That felt deeply wrong.

Attic started as a weekend script and grew into something I use every single day. The difference is simple: the index lives entirely on your machine, nothing phones home, and searches happen locally at speeds no cloud tool can match. It's your Mac doing what it was always capable of — you just needed the right key.

Quick start

Four steps. No account. No surprises.

01

Download and open Attic

Grab the free Mac app — it's under 5 MB. Drag to Applications, launch like any app. No installer, no setup wizard, no account required.

02

Let it index your files — once

Attic scans your chosen folders and builds a local index. This happens one time, stays entirely on your Mac, and never uploads a single byte to any server.

03

Search with natural language

Press ⌘K from anywhere. Type what you remember — a word from inside a doc, a project name, a file type. Results appear in under 50ms.

04

Your files stay on your Mac. Always.

No cloud calls. No background sync. No analytics. Every file you open is served directly from local storage — no middleman, no exceptions.
